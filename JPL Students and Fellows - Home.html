Your First Day

Congratulations on earning a stellar internship or fellowship at the leading center for robotic exploration of the solar system! Explore the resoures below to get started finding housing, arranging transportation and learning what to expect on your first day.



Badging

You will be receiving an automated email message from your program coordinator and IDMAX with instructions on how to complete your badging and registration applications. Note: These applications require that you provide personal information. You will need to complete both applications within seven days of receiving those emails.

Unitl you receive your official badge, you will be provided with a green temporary badge. Note: The green student badges do not operate the parking lot turnstiles, so you will have to walk to the nearest guard station to enter the laboratory.



Housing

JPL students and faculty are responsible for making their own housing arrangements. Traffic in the Los Angeles area is notoriously slow and unpredictable, so we recommend that you focus your housing search within about a 10-mile radius of the Laboratory, which is located just north of Pasadena in La Canada Flintridge. Local cities and communities in this region, known as the western San Gabriel Valley, include: Alhambra, Altadena, Arcadia, Burbank, Eagle Rock, Glendale, Highland Park, La Canada Flintridge, La Crescenta, Monrovia, Montrose, Pasadena, Rosemead, San Gabriel, Sierra Madre, South Pasadena, and Temple City.

Below is a list of resources that previous students and faculty have used to secure housing. Note: JPL does not endorse these resources.

Dorms, Student and Corporate Housing

  • Caltech Housing
    • Caltech housing is available to students in Caltech Student and Faculty Programs, or SFP, which includes the JPL Summer Internship Program, Space Grant, Caltech Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship, NASA Undergraduate Fellowship, and STEM Teacher and Research Program. Students must apply. Use the SFP Housing Application link in your SFP online account.
    • Caltech housing is not available for students in other programs in summer 2018 due to limited inventory from campus-housing renovations.

Transportation


Driving and Parking*Important parking notice for students

Most affiliates, including postdocs and interns will not be able to park at JPL, because JPL’s mission portfolio and corresponding workforce has grown beyond JPL’s available parking capacity. (Note: On your first day (orientation day), you will be able to park at JPL.

Rather than drastically reduce internship opportunities to mitigate the parking challenge, interns may choose from a variety of alternative transportation options listed in the dropdowns below.

We especially encourage you to use the JPL Interns-Fellow Facebook page now to organize carpools. If you would like to find an existing JPL carpool or vanpool, or connect with others who may be interested in carpooling, send an email to the JPL Rideshare Office at Rideshare@jpl.nasa.gov or John.Miranda@jpl.nasa.gov. Additional vanpooling and carpooling resources can be found at:

  • vRide: Create a Carpool
  • Enterprise: Ride Share Program
  • Metro: Participating Vanpools
  • Gas cost calculator

Public Transportation

  • From Downtown Los Angeles or the San Gabriel Valley
    • The Metro Gold Line connects to Pasadena from Downtown Los Angeles (heading northeast) and Monrovia (heading west).
    • The following buses also offer service to JPL from Pasadena. Students attending schools in Los Angeles County are eligible for a discounted pass.
      • Metro Bus 268
      • Metro Bus 177
      • Pasadena ARTS Bus
      • Pasadena Transit Route 52
      • LCF Shuttle
      • Glendale Beeline
  • Around Lab - See "JPL Shuttles and Buses" on the resources page for information about transportation around lab, to JPL's Woodbury location and to Caltech
  • Additional resources:
    • Metro transit passes, tap cards and bus schedules
    • Metro Rewards for alternative commuting
    • Metro ExpressLanes

Bicycling

The JPL Bicycle Club offers resources, events and group rides (bike trains) for those interested in commuting to JPL by bike. Visit the club page for more info.

There are bicycle racks across the Lab where you can securely lock your bike. (Note: Bicycles must be parked in designated bicycle racks and bike riders must wear safety helmets while on Laboratory property.)

For health safety, it's a good idea to check the local air quality index before commuting to JPL by bike.



Your First Day - Checklist

  • U.S. Citizens and Legal Permanent Residents – must present original proof of citizenship AND proof of identity.

    • For proof of citizenship, please provide one of the following: U.S birth certificate, current U.S. passport, Native American tribal document or Military Form DD214, Naturalization Certificate, Resident Alien (green) Card, or an unexpired foreign passport with a stamp from Immigration and Naturalization Service (INS). Note: security cards are no longer accepted as proof of citizenship.

    • For proof of identity, please provide one of the following: Driver’s License or a photo ID card issued by federal, state, or local government agencies (or other entities, providing the ID contains your name, date of birth, gender, height, eye color and address), school ID card with a photograph, military card or draft record, Native American tribal document.

  • Foreign Nationals – Foreign nationals will need a passport and a visa with a stamp in the passport if the visa is a waiver.
